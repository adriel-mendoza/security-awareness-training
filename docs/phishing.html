Phishing Indicators and Tips

  • Suspicious Email Address: Check the sender's email address. Phishing emails often use addresses that look similar to legitimate ones. (e.g., suspicious@phishing.com)
  • Urgent Subject Line: Be wary of urgent or alarming subject lines designed to create a sense of urgency. (e.g., Urgent: Update Your Account Information)
  • Suspicious Activity: Phishing emails often mention suspicious activity to scare you into taking immediate action. (e.g., suspicious activity)
  • Click the Link: Avoid clicking on links in unsolicited emails. Hover over links to see the actual URL before clicking. (e.g., click the link below)
  • Threats of Suspension: Phishing emails may threaten account suspension to prompt immediate action. (e.g., temporarily suspended)
